About Mexico

Mexico is the home of many advanced Amerindian civilizations, such as the Olmec, Toltec, Mayan and Aztec civilizations. Later on in the 16th century, the country was colonized by Spain. Three centuries later, Mexico gained its independence.

Nowadays, Mexico is a Federal Presidential republic and its current president is Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador. The country is also America's largest goods trading partner.

Environment

Current Issues

Some current issues include economic and social concerns over low wages, underemployment, and few opportunities for the indigenous population in the impoverished southern states.

Mexico's criminal organizations have fought to control criminal markets resulting in thousands of drug-related homicides and disappearances since 2007.

Major Rivers

The Rio Grande river mouth, which is shared with the U.S, is 3,057 km. The Colorado river mouth is also shared with the U.S and it is 2,333 km.

Water and recycling

The major watersheds are through Atlantic and Pacific Ocean drainage. The Atlantic Ocean at the Gulf of Mexico is 607,965 square km. The Pacific Ocean at the Gulf of California is 703,148 square km.

The major aquifier is the Atlantic and Gulf Coastal Plains Aquifier. The solid waste recycled annually is 2.566 million tons. The total renewable water resource is 461.9 billion cubic meters.

Industries

The industries in Mexico are food and beverages, tobacco, chemicals, iron and steel, petroleum, mining, textiles, clothing, motor vehicles, consumer durables and tourism .

labor force

As per 2021, the labor force is 56.132 million in Mexico. If you are looking at that number by occupation, 13.4% of those jobs are in agriculture, 24.1% are in industry, and 61.9% are in services.

People and Society

Languages

People who only speak Spanish are 93.8%, which makes up for most of the country. 5.4% of people speak Spanish and indigenous languages. People who only speak indigenous languages make up 0.6% of the country.

The indigenous languages include Mayan, Nahuatl, and other languages.

Religions

Roman Catholics make up 78% of Mexico's population. Protestant and/or evangelican Christians account for 11.2% of the population.

Other religions make up 0.002% and those unaffiliated with a religion are 10.6% of the population.

Population Distribution

As of 2022, the population growth rate is 0.51%. Most of the population is located in the middle of the country in Jalisco and Veracruz. A quarter of the population is located in or around Mexico City.

The median age of the country is 29.3 years with the median age for men at 28.2 years and females 30.4 years.
